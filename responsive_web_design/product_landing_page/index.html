Yoga is the path for union

Hatha Yoga

In a Hatha Yoga class, you move in a gentle way. The hatha yoga postures are designed to cultivate strength, flexibility, openness, balance, and relaxation. The classes typically consist of 50% active poses and 50% relaxing poses. Practising Hatha Yoga on a regular basis will allow prana (life force) to flow freely, reduce anxiety and stress, invite steadiness of breathing, clarity of mind, improve circulation and flexibility.

Yin Yoga

Yin Yoga combines aspects of Hatha Yoga, the concept of the meridians in Chinese medicine, Taoist philosophy and Buddhist meditation techniques. By holding the postures in a seated or lying position for 3 - 5 minutes, it directs the stimulation deeper into connective tissues, such as ligaments, bones, and the joints of the body. When practised regularly, Yin Yoga increases the flexibility, circulation, and range of motion in the joints and ligaments. In the class, you will find yourself relaxing from ‘the perfection’ of the world and enjoying the presence ‘as it is being mindful of your current state.

Vinyasa Yoga

In Vinyasa Yoga, you experience a flow from pose to pose, linked together by the breath. During this practice, you will smoothly transition through a unique sequence, building physical strength, endurance, and flexibility. We offer Vinyasa for beginners, but also have multiple options for intermediate or advanced practitioners who want to deepen and develop their practice.

Wanting to learn more and deepen her practice Sema completed teacher training at You Can Yoga in 2022, discovering that she had a fascination with energy channels in the body and a love of teaching others.
