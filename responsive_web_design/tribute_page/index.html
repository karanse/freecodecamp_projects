Mustafa Kemal ATATÜRK

Turkey's first President and the leader of the Turkish War of Independence

Mustafa Kemal ATATÜRK was a Turkish field marshal, revolutionary statesman, author, and the founding father of the Republic of Turkey, serving as its first president from 1923 until his death in 1938. He undertook sweeping progressive reforms, which modernized Turkey into a secular, industrializing nation. Ideologically a secularist and nationalist, his policies and socio-political theories became known as Kemalism. Due to his military and political accomplishments, Atatürk is regarded as one of the most important political leaders of the 20th century.

A Brief Summary of His Life

  • 1881 - Mustafa Kemal Atatürk is born in Salonika.
  • 1893 - The young Mustafa enters the Military Preparatory School in Salonika and is given the second name "Kemal" by his teacher.
  • 1895 - Mustafa Kemal enters the Military High School at Manastir.
  • 1899 - Mustafa Kemal enters the infantry class of the Military Academy in Istanbul.
  • 1902 - Mustafa Kemal graduates from the Military Academy and continues his studies at the General Staff College.
  • 1905 - Mustafa Kemal graduates from the General Staff College with the rank of Staff Captain and is posted to the Fifth Army, based in Damascus.
  • 1906 - Mustafa Kemal and his friends become active in the little known "Fatherland and Freedom" society in Damascus.
  • 1907 - Mustafa Kemal is transferred to the Third Army and travels to Salonika.
  • 1911 - Mustafa Kemal is transferred to the General Staff in Istanbul.
  • 1912 - Mustafa Kemal successfully leads the Tobruk offensive in Libya.
  • 1912 - Mustafa Kemal is appointed Director of Operations (Mediterranean Straits Special Forces).
  • 1913 - Mustafa Kemal is appointed Military Attaché in Sofia.
  • 1915 - Allied navy fails to force the Turkish Straits. Mustafa Kemal demonstrates outstanding skills as a soldier and commander throughout the battle at Canakkale.
  • 1915 - Allied troops land at Arıburnu (Anzac Cove) along the Dardanelles; Mustafa Kemal plays a major role in stopping their advance.
  • 1915 - Mustafa Kemal is appointed Commander of the Anafartalar Group on the Gallipoli Peninsula
  • 1916 - Mustafa Kemal is promoted Brigadier-General.
  • 1916 - Mustafa Kemal wins Bitlis and Muş back from the enemy.
  • 1918 - Mustafa Kemal becomes Commander, 'Lightning Group' of Armies.
  • 1919 - Mustafa Kemal is appointed Inspector of the 9th Army based in Erzurum and is granted extensive powers.
  • 1919 - Mustafa Kemal leaves Istanbul for Samsun on the Bandırma vessel.
  • 1919 - Mustafa Kemal lands in Samsun, a city located on the Black Sea.. This date marks the beginning of the Turkish national liberation movement, and is an official holiday dedicated to the youth of Turkey.
  • 1919 - Mustafa Kemal resigns from his post of Inspector of the 3rd Army, and from the army.
  • 1919 - Mustafa Kemal is elected Chairman of the Erzurum Congress.
  • 1919 - Mustafa Kemal is elected Chairman of the Sivas Congress.
  • 1920 - Mustafa Kemal opens the Turkish Grand National Assembly in Ankara.
  • 1920 - Mustafa Kemal is condemned to death by the government in Istanbul.
  • 1921 - Mustafa Kemal is appointed Commander-in-Chief by the Grand National Assembly.
  • 1921 - The battle of Sakarya, led by Mustafa Kemal, begins.
  • 1921 - The Grand National Assembly gives Mustafa Kemal the rank of Marshal and the title Gazi (Victor).
  • 1922 -Gazi Mustafa Kemal begins to lead the Great Offensive from the hill of Kocatepe.
  • 1922 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal Paşa wins the battle of Dumlupınar.
  • 1922 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal enters Izmir.
  • 1922 - The Grand National Assembly accepts Gazi Mustafa Kemal's proposal to abolish the Sultanate.
  • 1923 - Proclamation of the Turkish Republic. Gazi Mustafa Kemal is elected Turkey's First President.
  • 1924 - As part of Turkey's modernization, Gazi Mustafa Kemal wears a hat at Sarayburnu in Istanbul.
  • 1928 - As part of Turkey's reform process, Gazi Mustafa Kemal educates the public on the new Turkish alphabet at Sarayburnu.
  • 1931 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal establishes the Turkish Historical Society.
  • 1932 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal founds the Turkish Linguistic Society.
  • 1934 - The Grand National Assembly passes a law granting Gazi Mustafa Kemal the surname "Atatürk."
  • 1938 - November 10 - Atatürk dies.
